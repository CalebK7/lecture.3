Shoes Hall of Fame

Caleb Ketema: Graphic Artist - Web Designer - Illustrator

My oppinion of shoes that belong in the hall of fame because they changed how shoes are today.

Nike Air Jordan 1

The original Air Jordan sneakers were produced exclusively for Michael Jordan in late 1984, and released to the public on April 1, 1985.

Nike Air force 1

Designed by Bruce Kilgore and introduced in 1982, the Air Force 1 was the first ever basketball shoe to feature Nike Air technology, revolutionizing the game and sneaker culture forever.

Air Penny 2

The Nike Air Penny was originally released in 1996 and has been retroed several times since then.

Chuck Taylor All-Star

Converse started making an early basketball shoe in 1917 and redesigned it in 1922, when Chuck Taylor asked the company to create a better shoe with more support and flexibility. After Converse added Taylor's signature to the ankle patch they became known as Chuck Taylor All Stars.

Air Jordan XI

11's non-traditional look largely comes down to one factor: its shiny, wraparound patent leather upper. In spite of this, the 11 is one of the most globally appreciated and collectible sneakers in the Jumpman canon, and it happens to be Michael Jordan's favorite model.

KB8, Crazy 8

The Adidas Crazy 8 was originally released in 1997 as the KB8. Their name was changed when they were retroed for the first time in 2007 due to Kobe no longer being with the brand.
